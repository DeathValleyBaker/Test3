A list of the Ingredients!

This page will talk about each ingredient that goes into a cookie and what they do to it.

Butter

One of the wet ingredients that will be used first. Butter is a base ingredient and very important for the taste and spread of the cookie. Butter has a high fat-to-water ratio. The fat content will keep the dough from being too liquidy. It is preferred to use unsalted butter over salted butter, but the difference is almost non-existant.

Sugar

The second wet ingredient added to butter. There are three types of sugar; pure cane sugar, light brown sugar, and dark brown sugar. The difference between the three would be the addition of molasses. Molasses will affect the spread of the cookie. It's best to use a combination of white sugar and brown sugar for a balanced cookie.

Eggs

Eggs are a binding ingredient, and the last wet ingredient. Cookies are best when using the whole egg. Using only egg whites will cause problems in mixing and gave dense cookies. Using only egg yolks yielded poor spread and dry cookies.

Flour

Flour is your dry ingredient, and the ingredient that is added last. The most popular flour for cookies is all-purpose flour. It helps the cookies rise in the oven and also contributes to the cookie's texture.

Leaveners

Leaveners are added with the flour. Baking soda is the leavener of choice in most recipes and is used with an acid ingredient; sugar. Baking soda promotes rise in the oven and lets cookies spread.

Extras

The minor, but still very necessary, ingredients are salt, vanilla extract (makes it a little bit tastier), espresso powder (if you're fancy), and chocolate chips (if you want to). Add little or no salt if using salted butter.
