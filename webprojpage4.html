Extra Information that needs to be heard.

Just a bit of tidbits that are good to know.

Dropping the Pan Against the Counter (a.k.a) Wacking it

When taking the cookies out of the oven, it's recommended to drop it against the counter. This will interrupt the baking within the cookie and give them beautiful cracks and fissures.

Browned Butter

One way to step up your baking game would be to go the extra mile and brown the butter. Whipping out a pan and cooking up the butter will give it an intense, deep and rich taste. A definite flavor booster.

Use Light-Colored Pans

Dark-colored pans will draw in more heat from the oven and burn the bottom of the cookies quickly. Use light-colored pans so the cookies bake evenly.

Use A Scale and Measure in Grams

If you had to choose between cups and grams, grams will always be the better pick. Grams are more accurate than cups since you're measuring weight rather than volume. There's nothing wrong with cups, but not all measuring cups are created equally.

Chill and Wait atleast 24-hours Before Baking

It's tempting to bake off the dough once it's been made, but hold off on baking! If you're willing to wait, put the dough in a bag and let it chill for a whole day. The ingredients will get to know each other better, giving you a richer flavor once you finally do bake them off. 30-minutes is the minimum, and 48-hours gives the best results.
