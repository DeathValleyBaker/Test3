Common substitutes that are used.

Sometimes you can't use certain ingredients, so you must change them.

Butter Substitutes

Instead of butter, you may use margarine or shortening. Shortening has much bigger fat-to-water content, and it lacks the strong flavor that butter brings. Margarine has more water-to-fat. Margarine will make your dough very soupy and cookies will spread a lot.

Sugar Substitutes

Stevia, honey, and maple syrup. Stevia is not designed to completely replace the sugar needed. So it's best to use half in the recipe, and add something else. Honey is an excellent binding ingredient, and maple syrup can add depth to cookies.

Egg Substitutes

You can use apple sauce, yogurt, or condensed milk. Use thick yogurt so the dough isn't runny. Apple sauce gives your cookies structure and adds texture. Condensed milk is a good binder and can give rich flavor.

Flour Substitutes

Substitutes for all-purpose flour would be cake flour and bread flour. The difference would be the protein content. Bread flour will make cookies more chewier, while cake flour will make it more softer and cakey.

Leavener Substitutes

Baking powder is the standard replacement for baking soda. Baking powder requires an extra ingredient to activate; which is usually cream of tartar. Baking powder makes cookies more dense. If using baking powder, it is important to cream your batter.
