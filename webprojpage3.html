Baking Mistakes that may happen.

Common mistakes that you might make while baking.

Over-Mixing the Batter

Be careful mixing the wet ingredients and the dry ingredients together for an extended period of time. Overduing the mixing could lead to adding too much air into the dough. This is fine for cakes, but not cookies. As this will cause cookies to take on a cakey and thick texture.

Burning the Butter

The process of making browned butter can go wrong very quickly. It's best to stop cooking the butter once it becomes slightly brown. The butter may not look brown at first glance, but if it has a slight brown hue, it's finished. It's very easy to go from browned to burnt. Burnt butter is NOT good.

Not Scraping the Bowl

When mixing the dough, it's important to make sure you scrape the bowl, so the ingredients mix evenly. Not doing this won't necessarily destroy your batter, but parts of the mixture will be stuck to the sides of the bowl and you won't get as good results.

Measuring Flour Incorrectly

If using cups to measure ingredients, do not plunge the cup into the flour bag to get the flour. This will pack in more flour than needed for the recipe. Use a spoon to scoop the flour into the cup and use the side to level out the extra flour.

Not following the Recipe

It's easy to feel like the recipe uses too much sugar, so you cut back on it. Or skip out on the cream of tartar because you just don't have any. This, more often than not, will lead to a baking fail. Always follow the recipe for your best possible chance at successful cookies.
